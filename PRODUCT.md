# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Churchill & Sejal, planning trips together (currently a New Mexico trip). They use this on
the go and while planning ahead of time — scanning a shared list to decide where to eat next,
filtering by dietary needs, city, or whether a spot needs a reservation.

## Product Purpose

A shared dining guide for trip planning: browse and filter a curated list of restaurants by
cuisine, city, dietary tags (vegan/vegan-friendly, carnivore), reservation requirements, and
Chase Sapphire OpenTable credit eligibility. Add new restaurants, mark ones as visited, and
quickly find "where should we eat" without re-researching or re-litigating the decision.
Success looks like: opening the guide mid-trip and deciding in seconds, not minutes.

## Brand Personality

Playful & vibrant — this is a personal trip-planning tool, not a corporate app. It should feel
like a well-loved travel notebook: warm, a little fun, full of personality (emoji badges,
distinct colors per tag), and quick to scan. Avoid generic SaaS/dashboard sterility.

## Anti-references

Enterprise admin dashboards, generic SaaS list views, sterile card grids. Nothing that feels
like it's trying to sell something — this has no audience but the two of them.

## Design Principles

- Scannable over exhaustive — dietary/reservation/city info should be readable at a glance via
  color and iconography, not paragraphs.
- Personal, not corporate — favor warmth and a bit of playfulness over enterprise polish.
- Filtering is the core interaction — search and filters must stay fast and low-friction as the
  list grows.
- Mobile-first practicality — this gets used standing in a parking lot deciding where to eat.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA: ≥4.5:1 body text contrast, ≥3:1 for large text, full keyboard navigation,
visible focus states. No additional requirements beyond good defaults.
